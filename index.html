Three things I've built.

01 — Prediction engine

Predicted a 15km ocean swim
to within 90 seconds.

Open water crossings in Cape Town involve cold Atlantic water, tidal streams, wind-driven currents and pace degradation over distance. I built a multi-variable prediction engine from scratch — integrating live tidal feeds, marine weather data, cold water pace penalty curves and GPS-calibrated feed-stop modelling. No vendor. No off-the-shelf model.

"5:23:30 — your app in 1m:30sec out. Bloody unbelievable. You are a GURU of note." Open water swimmer — after completing her 15km Robben Island double crossing
02 — Consumer mobile app

Zero to 450 users
in four weeks.

South Africa's open water swimming community had no platform for real-time conditions, group coordination or community tracking — just WhatsApp groups. I built SwimLoading: a React Native app with live conditions, GPS swim logging, leaderboards and race organisation. Self-funded. Zero marketing. Built and shipped solo. Four regions in eight weeks, with a Maurten sponsorship following.

"Dave Welensky verifying my day conditions with an unbelievable app — check it out swimmers." Yolanda Carstens — open water swimmer, SA community
03 — Enterprise data platform

171,000 data failures
caught before go-live.

A large South African retailer was migrating to SAP S/4HANA with 200,000 master data records across 993 columns. I built a proprietary data quality engine from scratch — no vendor, no off-the-shelf tooling — running daily in production, surfacing every failure and routing it to the right business owner before it reached the new system.

01 — Prediction engine
The engineering challenge

I built a model that predicted a 15km ocean swim to within 90 seconds.

Open water swimming in False Bay and around Robben Island involves cold Atlantic water (12–16°C), unpredictable tidal streams, variable wind and significant pace degradation over distance. I built a prediction engine from scratch — no vendor, no off-the-shelf model — that combines live tidal feeds, Open-Meteo marine data, cold water pace penalty curves and feed-stop modelling, calibrated on real GPS data from elite athletes.

The result: a swimmer entered the water for a 15km Robben Island double crossing. My model predicted 5 hours 20 minutes. She swam 5:23:30.
